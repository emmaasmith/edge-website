Our mission


Edge is the umbrella organization for undergraduate entrepreneurship activity at UChicago.

Our mission is to provide UChicago students with resources and a community to help them build skills, companies, and relationships.

What does Edge do?

We have four tracks that run programming in different areas of entrepreneurship.

If you're unsure where you fit in, click here to learn how Edge -- and the greater UChicago entrepreneurial community -- can help you.

Learn about our tracks below, and get involved with one (or more)!

Labs: start something

Edge Labs welcomes students interested in starting their own businesses while at the University of Chicago. Labs provides mentorship, guest speakers, and extensive training in relevant skills.

The track also helps student companies navigate the College New Venture Challenge hosted by the UChicago Career Advancement Office in spring.

Venture Capital: consult & invest

Midway Ventures allows students to learn hands-on about VC, offering pro-bono advisory services for startups.

Midway assists its clients with tasks including growth and marketing strategy, due diligence, and pitch deck creation. Midway also partners with leading Chicago-based venture capital firms to source and evaluate startups.

Academy: learn skills

Academy offers classes in a number of practical subjects that are important for anyone -- especially those interested in the intersection of technology and entrepreneurship.

From design to web development, Academy explores skills relevant both to creating one's own business and to excelling in our modern, digital society.

Open Events: network and hear from experts

Edge hosts a number of major events that are open to the public. Such events include:

  1. Large-name speakers (e.g. Sam Yagan, Peter Thiel)
  2. Site visits to Chicago-area companies (e.g. GroupOn)
  3. Phoenix Pitch (our pitch competition).
